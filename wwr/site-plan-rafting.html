Rafting Site Plan

Cinthia Paez

Overview

Purpose

Dry Oar Rafting Co.'s online presence is a digital gateway to the thrill of white-water rafting. The website will: Generate revenue by driving bookings and promoting the business to a wider audience. Provide information about the company's rafting experiences, safety measures, and booking options. Build community by connecting rafters and sharing stories and tips. The website will be a living, breathing testament to Dry Oar's passion for the river.

Audience

Who are the target customers? Describe their life (or business) situation (age, financial situation, interests, and so on) Thrill-seekers and nature lovers aged 18-55 with moderate disposable income and a sense of adventure. They lead active lifestyles and crave new experiences that challenge them physically and connect them with nature. Young professionals: Looking for adrenaline-pumping activities to break from their routine and reconnect with friends. Active families: Seeking exciting outdoor adventures that everyone can enjoy together, creating lasting memories. Solo travelers: Eager for unique and challenging experiences that combine self-discovery with stunning landscapes. Outdoor enthusiasts: Passionate about nature and physical activity, always looking for new ways to explore the world. What do they expect to find or what questions will be answered by them accessing the website? Detailed descriptions and difficulty ratings of different rafting trips. Thrilling photos and videos showcasing the excitement of whitewater rapids. Easy online booking and last-minute availability for spontaneous adventures. Social media integration to share their experience with friends and family. Family-friendly rafting trips with calm water and gentle rapids suitable for all ages. Safety information and equipment specifically designed for children. Photos and videos of families having fun on the river to spark interest. Engaging activities and educational resources to keep children entertained throughout the trip. Calmer rafting trips with scenic views and opportunities for quiet reflection. Guided meditation or yoga sessions offered on some trips for deeper connection to the environment. Blogs or stories written by solo rafters sharing their experiences of finding peace on the water. Information about nearby eco-friendly accommodations or camping options. What are their needs that aren't being met? Basic rafting websites often lack the thrill factor or detailed information to truly excite adventurous groups. Mobile-friendliness and social media integration are crucial for spontaneous planning. Many rafting websites focus on adrenaline-pumping adventures and lack information about family-friendly options. Kid-friendly activities and safety information are crucial for parents. Rafting websites often cater to groups or adrenaline seekers, neglecting the solo traveler seeking serenity. Information about peaceful trips and opportunities for self-reflection are key. How will my audience usually access my site (desktop, laptop, mobile device)? Laptops and mobile devices are likely her primary access points, used for research and booking.
